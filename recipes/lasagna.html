Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a timeless Italian dish with little room for failure. Beginners love to cook a lasagna because they are extremely easy to make, yet extremely delicious. You can get fancy by adding different cheeses and spices, or you can keep it classic with the good ol' ground beef-mozzarella-tomato sauce combo. In today's recipe we're sticking with the tried and true trio.

Ingredients

  • 1 and 1/2 pounds ground beef
  • 2 (6 oz.) cans of tomato sauce
  • 2 (6 oz.) cans of tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup of water
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tbsp salt, or to taste
  • 1 pound mozzarella cheese (shredded or sliced)
  • 12 Lasagna noodles

Instructions

  1. Cook your beef:
    Add olive oil to a large pan, keep heat on high and add ground beef. Cook until brown.
  2. Make your sauce:
    In the same pan, add all of your tomato sauce, while slowly adding in your tomato paste, mixing with water.
  3. Preheat:
    Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.
  4. Assemble:
    In a baking dish, start by adding a layer of meat sauce, followed by a layer of mozzarella, followed by a layer of noodles. Repeat this twice more, then do one more layer of sauce, and one more layer of cheese.
  5. Bake:
    Place baking dish in oven and put a timer for 25 minutes, remove foil, and bake for another 25 minutes.
